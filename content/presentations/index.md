# Methylphenidate and Cue-Evoked Heart Rate in Co-morbid Alcohol Use Disorder and ADHD

**Poster Presentation**  
University of Florida CARE Annual Symposium • Spring 2026 Undergraduate Research Symposium

## Research Overview

Presented research examining how methylphenidate influences cue-evoked cardiovascular responses in young adults with co-occurring Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD) and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). Using heart rate and heart rate variability data collected during a double-blind, placebo-controlled experiment, the project applied statistical modeling to characterize physiological responses across medication and cue exposure conditions.

## Research Contributions

As an Undergraduate Research Assistant in the Neurological Aspects of Addiction Laboratory, I developed reproducible analysis workflows for physiological time-series data and contributed to the statistical analysis supporting this research.

My work includes:

- Processed heart rate and HRV data collected during fMRI experiments
- Developed reproducible R and Python pipelines for physiological signal processing
- Implemented feature extraction and quality-control procedures
- Applied linear mixed-effects models to evaluate experimental effects
- Created publication-quality visualizations to communicate findings
- Assisted with interpretation of statistical results in collaboration with the research team

## Key Findings

- Alcohol-related cues elicited measurable changes in cardiovascular activity.
- Methylphenidate significantly increased heart rate relative to placebo.
- Statistical modeling helped characterize physiological responses across repeated experimental conditions.

## Statistical Methods
