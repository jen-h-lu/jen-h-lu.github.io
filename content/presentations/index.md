# Methylphenidate and Cue-Evoked Heart Rate in Co-morbid Alcohol Use Disorder and ADHD

**Poster Presentation**  
University of Florida CARE Annual Symposium  
Spring 2026 Undergraduate Research Symposium

## Research Overview

Presented research investigating physiological responses associated with alcohol cue exposure and methylphenidate treatment in individuals with co-morbid Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD) and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).

The study examined heart rate responses collected during a double-blind, placebo-controlled experimental design, using statistical approaches to characterize physiological changes across experimental conditions.

## Research Contributions

As an Undergraduate Research Assistant in the Neurological Aspects of Addiction Lab, I contribute to the analysis of physiological data collected during addiction neuroscience studies.

My work includes:

- Processing and analyzing heart rate and heart rate variability (HRV) data
- Developing reproducible data processing pipelines using R and Python
- Applying statistical modeling approaches to evaluate physiological responses
- Visualizing physiological patterns across experimental conditions
- Supporting interpretation of findings within addiction neuroscience research

## Statistical Methods

- Heart rate variability analysis
- Physiological time-series processing
- Linear mixed-effects modeling
- Repeated-measures analysis
- Experimental data modeling
