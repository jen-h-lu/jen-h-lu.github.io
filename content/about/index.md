# About Me

I am an undergraduate Statistics student at the University of Florida pursuing research at the intersection of statistical learning, artificial intelligence, and biomedical data science.

My interest in statistics began with a simple question: **why?** Rather than viewing statistics as a collection of procedures, I became interested in understanding why methods work, when assumptions are justified, and how statistical reasoning can transform complex data into reliable scientific insights.

Through experiences spanning neuroscience, computational biology, machine learning, and industry, I have developed a passion for creating statistical methods that are not only predictive, but also interpretable and scientifically meaningful.
